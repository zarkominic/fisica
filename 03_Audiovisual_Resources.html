Carefully selected videos & simulations · 3rd ESO level

How to use this page

Watch these videos in order to support your study. After each video, try to answer the questions written below it. The PhET simulations at the end let you "play" with the gas variables and see the laws in action.

💡 Tip: turn on YouTube subtitles (CC) if you need extra help with English vocabulary.

2 · Boyle-Mariotte Law (P–V)

Home-made experiments — Boyle's Law

BoyleP–V

Several simple experiments showing that P and V are inversely related when T is constant.

Think about: What happens to the marshmallow inside the syringe when you compress it?

3 · Gay-Lussac's Law (P–T)

Gay-Lussac — candle & glass demonstration

Gay-LussacP–T

Combustion heats the air, pressure rises. When the flame goes out, T drops, P drops, and atmospheric pressure pushes water up into the glass.

Think about: Why does the water rise only after the flame goes out?

4 · Charles's Law (V–T)

Balloon experiment — hot vs cold water

CharlesV–T

In hot water, the air inside the bottle expands and the balloon inflates. In cold water, the air contracts and the balloon deflates.

Think about: What law is this an example of? Why must T be in kelvin?
